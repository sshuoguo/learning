Optimization Theory

Introduction to linear Optimization, Bertsimas, Dimitris, and John N. Tsitsiklis.
Highly recommended
The book starts from linear programming and gradually uncovers duality. It provides a clear and intuitive exploration of why duality arises within the LP framework, before later extending the discussion to Lagrangian duality.
What makes the book particularly remarkable is that, although it is built around linear programming, it naturally introduces more advanced perspectives in the later chapters. For example, it discusses interior-point methods and touches on fundamental ideas in integer linear programming, effectively bridging linear and nonlinear optimization viewpoints.
Overall, this book reflects the depth and clarity of its authors—two leading researchers in operations research. It offers beginners a rare opportunity to quickly gain a structured and meaningful view of the landscape of optimization theory.
Finally, a note: optimization cannot be learned passively. This book contains a large number of exercises, some of which are quite challenging. Working through them seriously—especially early in one’s academic journey—can be extremely valuable for long-term research development.
