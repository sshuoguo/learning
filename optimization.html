Introductory Learning
An Overview of Gradient Descent Optimization Algorithms
[PDF]
Sebastian Ruder
Covers GD, SGD, NAG, Adam, and other important first-order methods. A good place to begin.
However, this overview does not discuss convergence rates or error analysis.
Smooth Functions
[PDF]
Aaron Sidford (course notes)
A quick introduction to L-smoothness and the gradient method. You will see basic bounds and convergence rates.
Linear Convergence of Gradient and Proximal-Gradient Methods Under the Polyak–Łojasiewicz Condition
[PDF]
Hamed Karimi, Julie Nutini, and Mark Schmidt
A good warm start.
The appendix is especially important, and one can even start from there.
Introductory Online Courses on Optimization
[Courses]
Sergiy Butenko
Covers introductory optimization, Operations Research I, linear programming, integer programming (partially), and survey-style material.
Xingyu Zhou's Blog
[Website]
Xingyu Zhou
A very good refresh.
A collection of concise research notes on optimization and learning theory, covering topics such as differential privacy, convex analysis, and bandit algorithms.
UCLA Teaching Material
[Website]
Lieven Vandenberghe (course materials)
More generic course material, but still useful.
Optimization Theory
Introduction to Linear Optimization
[PDF]
Dimitris Bertsimas and John N. Tsitsiklis
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
Highly recommended.
The book starts from linear programming and gradually uncovers duality. It provides a clear and intuitive exploration of why duality arises within the LP framework, and later extends the discussion to Lagrangian duality.
It also introduces more advanced perspectives in the later chapters, including interior-point methods and basic ideas in integer linear programming, effectively bridging linear and nonlinear optimization viewpoints.
Linear and Nonlinear Programming
[PDF]
David G. Luenberger and Yinyu Ye
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
An excellent book to begin with for readers interested in optimization algorithms.
It starts from linear programming, moves into nonlinear optimization, and offers a thorough treatment of several major algorithmic frameworks. The book also includes many concrete examples.
Convex Optimization
[PDF]
Stephen Boyd and Lieven Vandenberghe
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
Take it as a dictionary.
Lectures on Convex Optimization
[PDF]
Yurii Nesterov
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
For readers who want to pursue research in convex optimization.
It has both depth and breadth. The only potential difficulty is that it is written in a more abstract style, but the viewpoint is correspondingly higher.
Numerical Optimization
[PDF]
Jorge Nocedal and Stephen Wright
Recommendation: ★★★☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
For readers with some background in numerical analysis, this is also a book worth recommending.
It is one of the clearest references for classifying and introducing the major classical optimization algorithms.
However, it is not especially systematic in organization. It is better used as a reference book than read cover to cover.
Integer and Combinatorial Optimization
[PDF]
Laurence A. Wolsey and George L. Nemhauser
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★★★
This book is enough for combinatorial optimization.
Combinatorial optimization is difficult. Please be cautious before going deeply into this field.
Honorable Mentions
Robust Optimization
[PDF]
Aharon Ben-Tal, Laurent El Ghaoui, and Arkadi Nemirovski
Recommendation: ★★☆☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
A classic text by three pioneers of robust optimization, but not an ideal entry point for beginners.
Parts of the exposition are opaque, and the book no longer reflects many later developments, especially in distributionally robust and adaptive multi-stage optimization.
For most readers, papers may be a better starting point.
Network Flows: Theory, Algorithms, and Applications
[PDF]
Ravindra K. Ahuja, Thomas L. Magnanti, and James B. Orlin
Recommendation: ★★★☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
An encyclopedic reference on network flows by three major scholars in the field.
Its ambition is impressive, but the first edition contains a substantial number of errors, and no definitive revised edition has appeared.
Best used as a reference book, and read with some critical caution.
Introduction to Online Convex Optimization
[PDF]
Elad Hazan
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
A short and elegant introduction to a highly active area.
The notes combine basic convex optimization, stochastic processes, and ideas from statistical learning, making them a particularly attractive entry point for readers who want to see the appeal of interdisciplinary optimization.
Lectures on Modern Convex Optimization
[PDF]
Aharon Ben-Tal and Arkadi Nemirovski
Recommendation: ★☆☆☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
A famously difficult book, perhaps the hardest optimization text I have read.
Its perspective is extremely high-level and offers a unified framework for understanding robust optimization and many deterministic optimization problems, but it is best suited to readers who actively want a serious technical challenge.
Lectures on Stochastic Programming: Modeling and Theory
[PDF]
Alexander Shapiro, Darinka Dentcheva, and Andrzej Ruszczyński
Recommendation: ★★★☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
A major work in stochastic optimization.
The book is demanding, and many of its examples are abstract, so readers will benefit from a solid background in analysis and probability, ideally including measure-theoretic probability.
Essential for readers seriously interested in stochastic optimization.
Finite-Dimensional Variational Inequalities and Complementarity Problems
[volume 1] [volume 2]
Francisco Facchinei and Jong-Shi Pang
Recommendation: ★★★☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
A foundational work on variational inequalities.
Since every convex optimization problem admits an equivalent variational inequality formulation, this viewpoint is often extremely powerful: it naturally encodes first-order information and can be analytically fruitful.
Even readers who do not study the whole book may benefit from learning this perspective.
Semidefinite Optimization and Convex Algebraic Geometry
[PDF]
Grigoriy Blekherman, Pablo A. Parrilo, and Rekha R. Thomas (eds.)
Recommendation: ★☆☆☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
A deep and specialized volume by major figures in semidefinite optimization.
Like variational inequalities, this is a field with fewer researchers but substantial depth.
It is best suited to readers with a strong algebraic background and is not a natural starting point for most beginners.
(Non)convex Analysis
Convex Analysis
[PDF]
R. Tyrrell Rockafellar
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★★★
One of the great classics on this list.
For optimization and operations research researchers, convex analysis can feel like a dragon-slaying art: abstract, difficult, but immensely powerful.
Even today, many lemmas and theorems from this book continue to generate new research across optimization, OR, management science, mathematics, and computer science.
Variational Analysis
[PDF]
R. Tyrrell Rockafellar and Roger J-B. Wets
Recommendation: ★★★☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
A monumental follow-up that moves beyond convexity and develops a broad framework for nonconvex analysis.
Like Convex Analysis, it is a masterwork, but it is even more demanding and best approached by readers who already have some maturity in analysis and optimization theory.
Nonlinear Programming
[PDF]
Dimitri P. Bertsekas
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
Although the title suggests optimization, this book is in many ways also a book about analysis.
Bertsekas develops nonlinear programming with substantial analytical depth, making it valuable for readers who want more than a purely algorithmic treatment.
Convex Optimization Theory
[PDF]
Dimitri P. Bertsekas
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
A rigorous and analysis-heavy treatment of convex optimization.
Despite the title, this is closer in spirit to convex analysis than to a standard algorithms text, and is particularly rewarding for readers who prefer a more theoretical and abstract viewpoint.
Convex Optimization Algorithms
[PDF]
Dimitri P. Bertsekas
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
The most accessible of Bertsekas’s major convex optimization books.
The viewpoint remains analytical, but the presentation is more algorithmically oriented and easier to enter than his more theory-focused texts.
Probability for Optimization
Introduction to Probability
[PDF]
Dimitri P. Bertsekas and John N. Tsitsiklis
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆
A very accessible introduction to probability, suitable even for first-year undergraduates.
It avoids measure theory but does an excellent job of building probabilistic intuition, which is often just as important as formalism in early study.
Probability: Theory and Examples
[PDF]
Rick Durrett
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
A standard and highly respected measure-theoretic probability text.
For research-oriented readers, especially those who need martingale methods and modern probability, this is an excellent choice—though it asks for more mathematical maturity than many alternatives.
Bandit Algorithms
[PDF]
Tor Lattimore and Csaba Szepesvári
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
A very strong modern reference on multi-armed bandits, bringing together a large body of theoretical results in one place.
Bandits remain lively, with strong links to reinforcement learning and growing industrial relevance.
Introduction to Multi-Armed Bandits
[PDF]
Aleksandrs Slivkins
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
A more accessible alternative to Bandit Algorithms.
It is less comprehensive, but still a very good entry point before moving to the fuller treatment.
Dynamic Programming, Optimal Control, and Reinforcement Learning
Neuro-Dynamic Programming
[PDF]
Dimitri P. Bertsekas and John N. Tsitsiklis
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
One of the most underrated books in this area.
Long before deep reinforcement learning became fashionable, this book already developed a framework for approximating value functions and policies with function approximators.
It remains deeply relevant for readers who want a theory-oriented view of RL and dynamic programming.
Dynamic Programming and Optimal Control
[PDF]
Dimitri P. Bertsekas
Recommendation: ★★★☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
A comprehensive and influential treatment of dynamic programming and optimal control.
It is extensive and detailed, making it a standard reference, though some readers may prefer a shorter and more conceptually concentrated entry point.
Abstract Dynamic Programming
[PDF]
Dimitri P. Bertsekas
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
A concise and high-level treatment of dynamic programming from an operator-theoretic viewpoint.
For readers who want to understand what dynamic programming is really doing at a structural level, this is exceptionally insightful.
Approximate Dynamic Programming: Solving the Curses of Dimensionality
[PDF]
Warren B. Powell
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
A major book on approximate dynamic programming by one of the central figures in the field.
The tone is generally more approachable than Bertsekas, and it is a valuable companion for readers interested in large-scale sequential decision problems and approximation methods.
