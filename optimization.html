Optimization Theory

Bertsimas, Dimitris, and John N. Tsitsiklis. Introduction to linear optimization. Vol. 6. Belmont, MA: Athena scientific, 1997.
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
Highly recommended
The book starts from linear programming and gradually uncovers duality. It provides a clear and intuitive exploration of why duality arises within the LP framework, and extends the discussion to Lagrangian duality.
It introduces more advanced perspectives in the later chapters. It discusses interior-point methods and touches on fundamental ideas in integer linear programming, effectively bridging linear and nonlinear optimization viewpoints.
Luenberger, David G., and Yinyu Ye.Linear and nonlinear programming. Vol. 2. Reading, MA: Addison-wesley, 1984.
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
For anyone interested in optimization algorithms, this is an excellent book to begin with. It starts from linear programming, moves into nonlinear optimization and offers a thorough treatment of several major algorithmic frameworks. The book includes many concrete examples.
Boyd, Stephen, and Lieven Vandenberghe. Convex optimization. Cambridge university press, 2004.
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
Take it as a dictionary.
Nesterov, Yurii. Lectures on convex optimization. Vol. 137. Berlin: Springer International Publishing, 2018.
Recommendation: ★★★★★ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
For those pursuing research in convex optimization. It has both depth and breadth. The only potential difficulty is that it is written in a more abstract style. This book offers a higher-level perspective.
Nocedal, Jorge, and Stephen Wright. Numerical optimization. Springer Science & Business Media, 2006.
Recommendation: ★★★☆☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
For readers with some background in numerical analysis, this is also a book worth recommending. It is perhaps one of the clearest references available for classifying and introducing the major classical optimization algorithms.
However, the book is not systematic in its organization. Use it as a reference book and turn to specific chapters when needed.
