Introduction to Probability
[PDF]
Dimitri P. Bertsekas and John N. Tsitsiklis
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★☆☆☆
A very accessible introduction to probability, suitable even for first-year undergraduates.
It avoids measure theory but does an excellent job of building probabilistic intuition, which is often just as important as formalism in early study.
Probability: Theory and Examples
[PDF]
Rick Durrett
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★★
A standard and highly respected measure-theoretic probability text.
For research-oriented readers, especially those who need martingale methods and modern probability, this is an excellent choice—though it asks for more mathematical maturity than many alternatives.
Bandit Algorithms
[PDF]
Tor Lattimore and Csaba Szepesvári
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★★☆
A very strong modern reference on multi-armed bandits, bringing together a large body of theoretical results in one place.
Bandits remain lively, with strong links to reinforcement learning and growing industrial relevance.
Introduction to Multi-Armed Bandits
[PDF]
Aleksandrs Slivkins
Recommendation: ★★★★☆ Difficulty: ★★★☆☆
A more accessible alternative to Bandit Algorithms.
It is less comprehensive, but still a very good entry point before moving to the fuller treatment.
